About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher passionate about AI and brain science. Currently working at University of Bern analyzing neural data to understand intelligence.

I received my PhD from École polytechnique fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL) in Switzerland. My thesis focused on building computational models of early sensory processing in the brain. I have authored several publications on this topic.

In my free time I enjoy hiking in the Alps, photography, and playing chess.

Research Interests

My core research interests lie at the intersection of neuroscience, machine learning, and artificial intelligence. Specific areas I'm focused on include:

  • Neural coding and computation
  • Hierarchical sensory processing
  • Brain-inspired AI
  • Reinforcement learning
