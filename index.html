About Me

I am a scientist passionate about AI and brain science. Currently working at University of Bern, analyzing neural data to understand intelligence.

I received my PhD from École polytechnique fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL) in Switzerland. My thesis focused on understanding the dopamine dynamics in reward-based learning.

In my free time I enjoy hiking in the Alps, skiing and photography.

Research Interests

My core research interests lie at the intersection of neuroscience, machine learning, and artificial intelligence. Specific areas I'm focused on include:

  • Neural coding and computation
  • Hierarchical sensory processing
  • Brain-inspired AI
  • Reinforcement learning
