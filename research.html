Research Projects

Studying the Neural Mechanisms Underlying Chronic Pain

Neuronal Plasticity Group, Department of Physiology, University of Bern

Dec 2022 - Present

Studied the neural mechanisms of chronic pain and its relief, using miniscope, single cell RNA sequencing, spatial omics and other techniques, with a focus on the ACC-PAG circuitry.

Investigating the Neural Mechanisms in Reward-Based Learning

Laboratory of Behavioral Genetics&Laboratory of Sensory Processing, EPFL

June 2018 - Dec 2022

Investigated the neural mechanisms underlying the individual differences in reward-based learning tasks. The dopamine activity was chronically measured before and during the learning process of a whisker detection learning task using a combined behavioral & fiber photometry recording approach. A pre-lick dopamine activity dip was found in some animals before learning, which turned out to be predictive of their future learning performances.

Understanding the Late-Associativity in Auditory-Amygdala Circuits

CAS Key Laboratory of Brain Function&Diseases, USTC

Sept 2015 - Dec 2017

Investigated the temporal requirements for fear conditioning association by using a combined behavioral & optogenetics approach. In contrast to the tight temporal requirement for classical CS-US paired conditioning, a distant association was found to be formed between temporally distant stimuli when the CS is strong.

Developing a Psycho-Physiology Recording Platform for Rodents

Cell Electrophysiology Laboratory, Wannan Medical College

Nov 2010 - June 2013

Established a platform for synchronized recording of central neural activities and peripheral functions via simultaneous recording of the electrical signals from two brain regions and multiple physiological signs, such as ECG, EMG, Breath rate, skin conductance and body temperature. This platform provides an appropriate approach for exploring the mechanisms underlying addiction and other mental illness.
